Emerging & Alternative Energy Sources

Nadila Basilu

10 min read · March 7, 2025

thumb_up comment
bookmark_add ios_share more_horiz

Alternative energy sources means energy from natural and renewable sources. As the world faces growing there will be a shortage of non renewable energy sources in future. That is why we should research and improve emerging and alternative energy sources. Examples are, Geothermal Systems(EGS),Ocean Thermal Energy Conversation(OTEC),Hydrogen Fuel Cells ,Fusion Energy, Bio Fuels, Tidal and Wave Energy.

What is the special of Emerging and Alternative Energy Sources?

These energy sources aim to provide cleaner and more efficient than traditional coal, oil and natural gases. Also most of them release least percentage of Carbon. So they are environmental healthy. Not only that they can provide continuously.
Now let us consider one by one and how they help to a bright future.

Geothermal Systems (EGS)

Geothermal systems harness heat energy from beneath earth’s surface for various applications including power generation, heating and cooling. This energy comes from Earth’s core, where temperature is extremely high. Geothermal power plants use steam or hot water from underground reservoirs to spin turbines and generate electricity.

Advantages of Geothermal Systems

Geothermal Systems control heat extraction in various locations. It is a Carbon-free power source. Not only that it produces minimal percentage of green house gases. Geothermal energy is available year around. So it can produce large amount of electricity to fulfill needs. Also they have a long operational life, making them a durable investment.

Disadvantages of Geothermal Systems

Installing geothermal systems ( large scale power plants, ground-source heat pumps) requires high budget. Geothermal drilling and fluid exaction cause to earth quacks. Those systems need significant amount of water for operation , which can be issue in water scarce areas.

Geothermal systems require regular maintenance. So governments should maintain them regularly and improve technology to go deeper levels.

Ocean Thermal Energy Conversation(OTEC)

Ocean Thermal Energy Conversation is a renewable energy technology that harnesses the temperature difference between warm surface water and cold deep seawater to generate electricity. The temperature difference should be at least 20oC.

Below picture shows the main parts of an OTEC system.

Advantages of Ocean Thermal Energy Conversation

Main advantage of Ocean Thermal Energy Conversation is it produces zero Carbon emissions and also releases less green house gases. OTEC offers high reliability as ocean temperature remains relatively stable year around. OTEC plants have around 30-50 years lifespan. They located offshore. So they have minimal land usage. OTEC systems can be coupled with desalination processes to produce fresh water. This is useful for water scarcity regions.

Disadvantages of Ocean Thermal Energy Conversation

The initial setups of OTEC is very expensive. OTEC systems need a significant temperature difference (20oC) which is only available in tropical regions. The efficiency of OTEC systems is relatively low. Not only that pumping up cold deep sea water may disturb marine ecosystems and aquatic life. The constant exposure to sea water can lead to corrosion of equipment because water which includes salt increases the speed of corrosion. So it need a regular maintenance and it need a high budget. Countries such as Japan, USA and several Pacific Nations have explored OTEC as a viable energy solution. If technological advancements improve efficiently and lower costs , OTEC could play a significant role in renewable energy production.

Fusion Energy

Fusion energy is an advanced and highly anticipated technology. Unlike nuclear fusion, it works by combining light atomic nuclei such as Hydrogen isotops, to release energy.

Advantages of Fusion Energy.

Specially, fusion energy does not produce carbon emissions. It has highest potential efficiency (60%-70%). Unlike solar or wind power this does not depend on external conditions. Fusion provides an incredibly reliable and continuous power supply. It does not require large land areas compared to other energy sources.
The lifespan of fusion power plant is around 40-80 years. International Thermonuclear Experimental Reactor (ITER) project in France, are working to make fusion energy reality. If successful, fusion could produce global energy production, safe and clean electricity.

Disadvantages of Fusion Energy.

To sustain fusion reactions it needs extremely high temperatures(100 million oC). Making those temperatures in real world is harder than we think. The technological challenges that needs are challenging to achieve. Also the constructions of fusion can have environmental impacts. Building and maintaining the infrastructures for fusion reactions is costly.
Fusion energy technology is still in the experimental stage. Governments and researchers continue fusion energy research with estimate that it may become viable by the 2050 s. While there are challenges it may be a great step of human and it will be replace many of today’s traditional energy sources.

Hydrogen Fuel Cells

Hydrogen fuel cells generate electricity by converting Hydrogen and Oxygen into water through an electrochemical reaction, making them a clean and efficient energy source. Hydrogen fuel cells are comparable to fossil fuels but without harmful emissions.

Advantages of Hydrogen Fuel Cells

The environmental impact of hydrogen fuel cells is minimal if green Hydrogen is used. The land use requirements of hydrogen fuel cells are low. They can operate continuously for applications like backup power, transportation. Hydrogen can produce domestically and enhance energy security.

Disadvantages of Hydrogen Fuel Cells

Hydrogen fuel cells need a high cost due to expensive production and storage methods. The lifespan of fuel cell systems is 5-10 years and it is comparatively small. Hydrogen has a low energy density by volume. So it is difficult to store and transport. Hydrogen is highly flammable and to handle it safely requires specialized equipments. There are technical challenges to Hydrogen fuel cells.

Hydrogen powered vehicles are growing rapidly with companies like Toyota and Hyundai. They have a long life comparatively battery electric vehicles. The aviation industry is also exploring Hydrogen powered aircrafts. Although there are challenges it will be a huge advancement of energy sources.

Bio Fuels

Bio mass and bio fuels are also expanding as alternatives to fossil fuels specially, in transportation and heating. Advanced bio fuels like Ethanol, bio diesel and synthetic fuels can be derived from waste materials.

Advantages of bio fuels

Bio fuels produce lower CO2 emissions compared to fossil fuels. They are environmentally friendly and less toxic than fossil fuels. Production of bio fuels creates job opportunities in agriculture and bio fuels help to recycle and reduce waste materials.

Disadvantages of bio fuels

Producing bio fuels requires significant land areas. It also requires large amount of water. Not only that the production and maintenance can be more expensive. Also this depend on crop yields. So seasonal variability is a challenge for bio fuels.

Tidal and waves

Tidal and wave represent an alternative energy harnessing the power of ocean currents to generate electricity. This process uses the kinetic energy from the movements of tidal, which are cause by the gravitational forces of the sun and moon.

Advantages of tidal and waves

Tidal and wave represent an alternative energy harnessing the power of ocean currents to generate electricity. This process uses the kinetic energy from the movements of tidal, which are cause by the gravitational forces of the sun and moon.

Disadvantages of tidal and waves

Building power plants like under water turbines requires significant investment. They depend on tidal cycles. So it is not continuous. Harsh marine environments will cause to maintenance challenges and several technological challenges will overcome for efficiency. Those processes will pose disturbance for marine navigation routines.

While technology is still in its early stage countries like UK and Canada are proving its’ potential. If this become successful tidal and wave power could provide renewable energy source for coastal regions worldwide.

    • While these energy sources come with their own challenges , those energy sources will be valuable investments for the future.
